What's in it

No marketing — here's what the GLSL actually does.

Procedural sky

Analytic atmospheric gradient with a real sun & moon disc, sunset reddening, twinkling stars, and time-of-day color that the fog and water pick up.

Soft shadows

Distorted shadow map with contact-hardening PCF — crisp up close, softer with distance. Translucent blockers (water, stained glass) tint the sunlight instead of fully blocking it.

Reflective water

Depth-based clarity (shallow reads clear, deep reads dark), layered moving waves, a fresnel sky reflection, and a bright sun sparkle.

Soft, sky-lit shading

Half-Lambert diffuse and luminous sky-colored ambient, so shadows read airy rather than black. Foliage glows when backlit; grass and leaves sway in the wind.

Atmosphere & post

Distance + height fog, optional volumetric god rays and bloom, ACES tone mapping, and a gentle filmic grade. Underwater and lava tints included.

Built to be tuned

An in-game settings screen with six performance profiles plus sliders for shadows, sun, fog, bloom, exposure and more — change anything without editing files.

Install

Two minutes. You need OptiFine or Iris first.

  1. Install Iris (recommended) or OptiFine for your Minecraft version.
  2. Launch the game and open the Shader Packs screen (Iris) or Options → Video Settings → Shaders (OptiFine).
  3. Click Open Shader Pack Folder.
  4. Drop the downloaded Plixar-Shaders.zip into that folder.
  5. Select Plixar Shaders in the list and apply. Adjust the profile in the options if you want more FPS or more eye-candy.

Not getting more than ~60 FPS? That's almost always a frame cap, not the pack — turn off VSync in Video Settings, and check that AMD/NVIDIA software isn't capping the framerate (e.g. Radeon Chill / Frame Rate Target). Lowering render distance helps most on integrated graphics.
